How Often Should You Water Succulents?

AD
Arjun Dev Plant Doctor · Published 12 Jul 2026 · 4 min read
Article Cover Photo
Quick answer: water only when the soil is completely dry — roughly every 10–14 days indoors. More succulents die from overwatering than underwatering.

Succulents are drought-tolerant by nature — which means the single biggest mistake new plant parents make is watering them too often. Here's the simple rule we teach every customer at Golden Aura.

💧 The "Soak and Dry" Method

Water thoroughly until it runs out of the drainage hole, then let the soil dry out completely before watering again. For most indoor succulents in our climate, that's roughly once every 10–14 days.

🚱 Signs of Overwatering

Mushy, translucent leaves, a soft stem, or soil that never fully dries are all signs of overwatering — the most common cause of succulent death we see at our plant doctor desk.

🌵 Signs of Underwatering

Wrinkled or shriveled leaves usually mean it's time for a deep soak. Succulents recover from underwatering far more easily than overwatering.

✅ Our Recommendation

When in doubt, wait a few more days. And always use a pot with a drainage hole — paired with our terracotta pots, which breathe well and help prevent root rot.
